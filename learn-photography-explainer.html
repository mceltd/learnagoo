The Rule of Thirds is one of the simplest ways you can make your photos look better.

Imagine your photo divided into 9 equal squares, like a tic‑tac‑toe grid.

Where the lines cross, that’s where your viewer’s eyes naturally go.

On the left, the cat is in the middle - that’s the centered example.

On the right, the cat sits on one of the grid intersections - that’s the Rule of Thirds in action.

Placing your subject off‑center like this makes your photos feel more balanced, interesting, and professional.
